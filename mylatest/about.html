About Us

It is past due that Muslims here in America [Minnesota in particular] start to concentrate and plan for the future. The youth today are the future leaders of our community, and should be the focus of our future planning.
It is imperative for the community to invest ample energy, resources and money to encourage and provide the youth with all the necessary skills needed to become exemplary human beings and representatives of this noble tradition.
Therefore, with the guidance of GOD Almighty, we have established the first ever scholarship exclusively for Minnesota Youth.

﻿﻿Our Vision

  • To foster Youth Leaders in Minnesota
  • To encourage active participation of the youth in Community Activities (volunteerism), outreach activities, and to be good citizens of the community.
  • To encourage higher education of future leaders.
  • To provide youth with additional monetary compensation so that they can spend time organizing youth oriented and community outreach activities rather than working low paying jobs to pay for their education.
  • To encourage youth to participate in activities that promote diversity and tolerance and to work towards eliminating prejudice and discrimination by educating the public on other cultures, with a focus on the Islamic and Muslim cultures.
  • This award has been established to assist, prepare and encourage the future leaders in our communities. This is intended to instill and encourage a spirit of community service in the youth along with their educational endeavors.

Rabia Mumtaz - President

I have been part of MYLA family since 2012; currently serving as the President. I have been previously involved with NWICC with their Youth and Communications committees. My other volunteer activities include serving at the Food Shelf organized by Building Blocks of Islam, serving at homeless shelter organized by Impact team of Qabeelat Madinatayn, volunteering at Interfaith iftars held by MAS, volunteering at other local events organized by local mosques/other organizations. In my free time, I focus on my very new and small event planning/event decorating business called Fancy Wrapper, or just sit by the fireplace (in winters) and crochet. I live in Blaine with my husband.

Syed Sumran Mohiuddin - Treasurer

I am currently the treasurer of MYLA. I graduated from the University of Southern California with a Master of Science in Biomedical Engineering in 2007 and have lived in Minnesota for the past 6 years. I currently work for Medtronic, Inc. and reside in Woodbury with my wife.

Ather Syed - Vice President and Scholarship Director

I currently work as a Business Analyst at Patterson Companies. I have undergrad in Finance/Account and finishing up my Masters in Computer Systems. I have been in Minnesota for the past 10 years. ​

Aisha Ahmed - Secretary

My name is Aisha Ahmed and I graduated from the University of Minnesota this past May (2015). I majored in Genetics, Cell Biology and Development, with a minor in Neuroscience. I am currently working at the Minneapolis Heart Institute Foundation (Abbott Northwestern Hospital) as a research coordinator for the valve team. InshaAllah I will be applying to medical school later in the year. I am also an Al-Amal school graduate, and therefore learned the importance of serving my community. I have volunteered for many non-profit institutions in the past, including ARAHA, ICM, Al-Amal School, Al-Shifaa clinic, and the UMN-TC Muslim Student Association. Alhamdulilah now that I have the time and resources, it is my turn to give back to the community that supported me. I wholeheartedly value the mission of MYLA and am excited to be on the board!

Reies Romero - Marketing 

​Hi my name is Reies Romero and I am a recent graduate of Augsburg College with a Bachelors in Social Work and a Saint Paul College Graduate in 2013 with an Associate’s Degree and it is also where I co-founded the MSA. I am the current leader of the Omega Zulu Twin Cities Chapter (UZN1973), and a founding member of the Universal Movement for the Advancement of Hip Hop. I am also the Director of the James Dewitt Yancey Foundation in MN and a facilitator with Restorative Justice Community Action. My focus is Social and Youth Justice, Hip Hop Activism and ending White Supremacy. I have been a DJ for close to 25 years and I also teach Hip Hop History, I have been an official speaker with the Islamic Resource Group for 3 years and I enjoy meaningful engagements with others about Islam. I like to travel, grill/cook, iron, read, learn new things, and attend rallies and protests. I am very easy to talk to and I am really into community building and engagement!
