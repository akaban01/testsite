Scholarship Recipient 2015-16

1. First time MYLA winner. Sara Osman is a rising junior at the University of Minnesota-Twin Cities. She is double-majoring in Global Studies and African/African-American Studies. She is the daughter of Somali immigrants. She plans on attending law school for human rights/civil rights and return to Somalia. She is passionate about education, equity and enjoys deconstructing white supremacy in her free time. Some of her accomplishments include: •2014 Coca-Cola Leaders of Promise Scholar •2015 All-Minnesota Academic Team honoree •Prepared and presented two workshops at the 2014 Overcoming Racism Conference •Published in “Story Circle Stories” by Rose McGee and Ann Fosco •MCTC Muslim Student Association President Spring 2015 •Panelist at the Annual Minnesota Malcolm X Conference 2014 & 2015 •Youth-In-Government Model United Nations college secretariat 2013-2015 •Civil Rights Intern for CAIR MN Spring 2015 •Project s.t.a.r.t. Leadership chair and peer mentor 2012-2015 •Recipient of 2015 Jack Kent Cooke Undergraduate Transfer Scholarship •Youth Leader for YUIR (Youth Undoing Institutional Racism) with American Friends Service Committee

2. First time MYLA winner. My name is Amineh Safi and I am a 2014 graduate from Augsburg College and hold a B.A. in Psychology and Political Science. In addition to being a 2012-13 Interfaith Scholar, I have been with the Minneapolis Building Blocks of Islam's Dawah Speakers Team since 2012. I have spoken about Islam in several high school and college classrooms, the Forgiveness 360 Project, Augsburg College's Interfaith Mini-Convo, MN Private College Scholars at the Capitol, the 2013 Nobel Peace Prize Forum's interfaith session, as well as established an Islamic class at Augsburg College alhamdoliLah. Moreover, I am an Al Maghrib Institute MN student, as well as their set-up lead volunteer. My aspiration in pursuing a Masters in Public Policy at the Humphrey School of Public Affairs is to gain the skills to consult with government officials and agencies on action steps that would enable Muslim and non-Muslim relations to flourish. Another goal is to convey the Muslim voice in politics and increase Muslim community participation. Ultimately, I aspire to promote change in community development policies to ensure they convey, respect, and preserve everyone’s rights.

8. First time MYLA winner. Zamzam Yusuf I am a transfer student entering my junior year at the University of Minnesota, Twin Cities. I'm double majoring in Sociology and Communication. I've come a long way in my academics. I went from failing half my classes in freshmen year of high school, to being one of the top two students at Minneapolis Community and Technical College (the school I transferred from). I've been given the honor to receive awards and be chosen to be a part of the All-Minnesota Academic Team and All-USA Academic Team of 2015. In terms of the future, I am an aspiring Youth worker. What got me interested in going down that route was when I worked as a camp counselor at Camp Sunrise last summer. This is a camp where urban youth between the ages of 13-18 from Minneapolis and St. Paul come and spend a week at camp. I worked there for 11 weeks. In the time that I spent at camp, I realized, finally, that this is what I want to do. I hope to serve, but not limited to, Somali youth. I feel like they are being underserved. I hope to also work with Somali parents in the hopes of bridging any generational/cultural gaps that may exist.
